Where does the news data come from?

The news stories are retrieved from the News API.

Where/How are the Sentiment scores achieved?

The project takes advantage the python package Vader Sentiment (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner). It's particularly attuned for lower word count blocks of text and because every story object contains a small, easily digestible description, Vader was the perfect choice. Prior to displaying the news items on the page, each individual story is filtered through the analyzer and three percentage scores are returned depending on that story's description phrasing. All scores are averaged and then returned for each batch of stories at the top of the two news containers for an overall representation .

Why create this project?

A news outlet can play a large roll in the way their individual readers view the world. It's no secret that the light (positive, neutral, or negative) a particular news segment is presented in is likely influenced by that outlet's own biases. The goal behind the project was to utilize a tool like Vader to attempt to quantify those biases so that the consumer can shape a more informed opinion.
